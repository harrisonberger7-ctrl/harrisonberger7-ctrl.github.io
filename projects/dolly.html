A folding material-handling dolly designed in Onshape and manufactured with CNC milling. The project combined mechanical design, detailed engineering drawings, component fabrication, assembly, and final demonstration.

Project overview

From CAD concept to working prototype

The dolly was developed as a complete mechanical system rather than a single machined component. The design uses paired tri-wheel assemblies to move over changes in elevation, a folding frame for storage, and custom linkage and locking features to control the deployed configuration.

My work included CAD design in Onshape, preparation of manufacturing drawings, CNC machining of custom parts, assembly, inspection, troubleshooting, and demonstration of the finished device.

Design development

CAD architecture and mechanical layout

Full assembly model

The Onshape model established the overall frame geometry, platform position, linkage locations, and tri-wheel arrangement before fabrication. Modeling the full assembly allowed component interfaces and motion to be evaluated before CNC manufacturing began.

Primary design features

  • Tri-wheel assemblies at the base for negotiating steps and raised surfaces.
  • A folding upper frame that reduces the dolly's stored footprint.
  • Custom joint and locking components connecting the frame sections.
  • A broad load platform supported by an aluminum frame structure.

Mechanical feature

Custom joint linkage

Folding-frame connection

The custom linkage joins sections of the frame and permits controlled movement between the collapsed and upright configurations. The mechanism was designed as part of the overall assembly rather than added after fabrication.

Iteration and troubleshooting

Learning from component failure

Prototype development included evaluating individual components after fabrication and use. The damaged gear-plate component shown here documents a real design and manufacturing issue rather than hiding it from the final project story.

Recording failures is valuable because it identifies where geometry, material thickness, loading, or manufacturing details may need to be reconsidered in a later revision.
